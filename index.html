Condorcet Internet Voting Service

Welcome to the Condorcet Internet Voting Service. This is an automated system that makes it easy to collect and summarize the opinions of a number of people on the internet on some topic of interest. Anyone is allowed to create a new CIVS election, and only authorized voters will know about it. You might use CIVS to:

  • Elect the president of an organization
  • Choose a name for a project
  • Elect a committee
  • Pick a restaurant
  • Rank the ten most favorite movies

Voters must have e-mail and web access. A voter is given a set of possible choices and is allowed to rank them. These rankings are combined using a Condorcet-based algorithm and used to report the ranking of the entire set of voters. When you create an election, voters are sent e-mail informing them that the election is open and giving them a URL where they can rank their choices. You can also create open elections for which voters do not need e-mail access; however, the results of such elections are much less trustworthy.

Because this voting system allows voters to rank their choices, rather than just giving their most preferred choice, it avoids problems like vote splitting and spoilers, and reduces the general problem of strategic voting. Australia is an example of a country that uses a voting system with ranked preferences, Instant Runoff Voting. The Condorcet voting system used by this service is better at identifying consensus candidates.
