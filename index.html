Condorcet Internet Voting Service

CIVS is a free Internet voting service that makes it easy to conduct polls on the Web. Each voter ranks a set of possible choices. Combined, these rankings are used to construct an overall ranking that anonymously summarizes the opinions of all voters. CIVS has been used for many polls and elections, including:

  • Officers of organizations
  • Meeting times
  • Members of committees
  • Project names, logos, domain names
  • Recipients of awards
  • Restaurants to eat at
  • Movies to watch
  • Party menus
  • Book club selections
  • Favorite music

How it works: Anyone may create a new CIVS poll, but only authorized voters will know about it. Voters and the poll supervisor must have e-mail and web access. When a poll is created, voters are e-mailed with a URL where they can vote. Public polls may be created that do not require voters to have e-mail addresses; in this case, one vote is allowed per IP address.

Preferential Voting: Preferential voting means voters rank their choices, rather than picking one choice. Compared to other polling methods, this collects and uses more information from voters. Preferential voting helps avoid vote splitting and spoilers. CIVS supports proportional representation, unlike other Condorcet systems.

Preferential voting systems are used now in real elections. For example, Australia uses Single Transferable Vote (STV). However, Condorcet voting methods are better at identifying consensus candidates. STV (and IRV) can elect a candidate even when a majority of voters would prefer someone else, and has in real elections.

A new, highly secure version of CIVS called Civitas is under development, and is the subject of a research paper. Civitas uses much more sophisticated cryptographic protocols to provide strong anonymity, universal verifiability, and coercion resistance.
