Condorcet Internet Voting Service

CIVS is a free Internet voting service that makes it easy to conduct elections and polls on the Web. A voter is given a set of possible choices and is allowed to rank them. Combined, these rankings are construct an overall ranking that anonymously summarizes the opinions of all the voters. For example, it has been used to:

  • Elect the president of an organization
  • Elect a committee
  • Choose a project name
  • Pick book club selections
  • Pick a restaurant to eat at
  • Identify the ten most favorite movies
  • Create a party menu
  • Select a meeting time

Here's how it works. Anyone is allowed to create a new CIVS election, and only authorized voters will know about it. Voters and the election supervisor must have e-mail and web access. When an election is created, voters are sent e-mail informing them that the election is open and giving them a URL where they can rank their choices. Web polls may also be created for which voters do not need e-mail access; one vote is allowed per IP address.

Because this voting system allows voters to rank their choices, rather than just picking their most preferred choice, it avoids problems like vote splitting and spoilers, and reduces the general problem of strategic voting. Australia is an example of a country that uses a voting system with ranked preferences, Instant Runoff Voting. The Condorcet voting system used by this service is better at identifying consensus candidates. Another name for Condorcet methods is Instant Round-Robin, because every candidate is compared against every other candidate, unlike Instant Runoff Voting where a consensus candidate can be eliminated in early rounds. CIVS also supports proportional representation.
